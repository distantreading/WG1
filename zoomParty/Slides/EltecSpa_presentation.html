Current situation

  • 81 novels (up to 6 874 582 tokens).

  • Balancing criteria satisfied:

    • Time slots: 16 novels from the first period (1840-1859), 15 from the second one (1860-1879) and 25 for each of the two last periods.
    • Author gender, 16 authors are women and 65 men.
    • Size balanced: 30 short novels (37%), 27 medium (33%) and 24 long novels (26%).
    • «Reprints» also balanced: 42 novels frequently reprinted (51%) and 39 rare novels (48%).

These days working on 7 novels for the first and second time slot. We hope to get up to 90 novels before the end of the project.

Text sources

  • Miguel de Cervantes Virtual Library (at the University of Alicante);
  • CLIGS corpus (at the University of Würzburg), mainly for the two last periods;
  • Hispanic Digital Library (Biblioteca digital hispánica) from the Spanish National Library (Biblioteca Nacional).

Compilation problems

Few problems.

  • Most novels are digitalized and normalized (modernized) at the BVMC. They deliver to us «for free».
  • It has been hard to find novels from the first period 1840-1859. There are few novels digitalized from this period.

Annotation problems

Language issue

Spain: a country with four official languages.

Two options:

  • Option 1, a sub-corpus for each language: ELTeC-SPA, ELTeC-CAT (Catalan), ELTeC-EUS (Basque) and ELTeC-GLG (Galician).

  • Option 2: to include in ELTeC-SPA novels writing in Catalan, Basque and Galician (at least one for each).

Language issue

At this moment option 1 is not possible for several reasons:

  1. These languages were recovered as “literay language” at the end of 19th Cent. It is not possible to fulfill balance criteria.

  2. Too much work for only one person.

I see this option as promising for further developments of ELTeC.

Language issue

Option 2 is more realistic.

Problems:

  • ELTeC-SPA will include several languages
  • The amount of novels in each period will not be balanced: there will be more novels in the last period.

Is it a problem for the global ELTeC corpus?
